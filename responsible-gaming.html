Our Commitment to Responsible Gaming

At Plinko, we believe that gambling should always be an enjoyable form of entertainment. We are committed to promoting responsible gaming practices and providing our users with the tools and resources they need to maintain control over their gambling habits.

We encourage all our users to approach gambling as a form of entertainment, not as a way to make money or solve financial problems. By setting limits, staying informed, and using the tools available, you can ensure that your gaming experience remains positive and enjoyable.

Signs of Problem Gambling

It's important to recognize the signs of problem gambling early. If you or someone you know exhibits any of the following behaviors, it may be time to seek help:

Spending more than you can afford

Gambling with money needed for essential expenses like bills, food, or rent.

Chasing losses

Continuing to gamble to recover money already lost, often with increasing bet sizes.

Neglecting responsibilities

Missing work, school, or family obligations due to gambling activities.

Lying about gambling habits

Hiding or lying about how much time or money is spent on gambling.

Gambling to escape problems

Using gambling as a way to escape stress, anxiety, or depression.

Borrowing money to gamble

Taking loans, using credit cards, or borrowing from friends and family to fund gambling.

Tools for Responsible Gaming

We provide several tools to help you maintain control over your gambling activities:

Deposit Limits

Set daily, weekly, or monthly limits on how much money you can deposit into your account.

Session Time Limits

Set limits on how long you can play in a single session, with notifications when you're approaching your limit.

Time-Out Period

Take a short break from gambling by setting a time-out period from 24 hours up to 6 weeks.

Self-Exclusion

Exclude yourself from our platform for a minimum of 6 months if you feel you need a longer break.

Activity Statements

Review your gambling history, including time spent, deposits, withdrawals, and game results.

Reality Check

Receive periodic notifications during gameplay showing time elapsed and net wins/losses.

Resources for Help and Support

If you or someone you know is struggling with gambling addiction, help is available. The following organizations provide support, counseling, and resources for problem gambling:

National Problem Gambling Helpline

24/7 confidential support for problem gamblers and their families.

1-800-522-4700

Gamblers Anonymous

A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope to solve their common problem.

www.gamblersanonymous.org

GamCare

Provides information, advice, and support for anyone affected by gambling problems.

www.gamcare.org.uk

Tips for Responsible Gaming

Set a Budget

Decide how much money you can afford to lose before you start playing and stick to that limit.

Set Time Limits

Decide how much time you want to spend gambling and set an alarm to remind you when to stop.

Balance Your Life

Make sure gambling doesn't interfere with or take priority over other activities and responsibilities.

Avoid Alcohol

Don't gamble under the influence of alcohol or drugs, as they can affect your decision-making.

Take Our Self-Assessment

Not sure if your gambling habits are healthy? Take our confidential self-assessment to evaluate your relationship with gambling.
